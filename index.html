‚ö†Ô∏è The Cross-Contamination Caution: Order Smart

While the ingredients themselves are safe, the fast-moving assembly line is the biggest risk for anyone with **Celiac Disease** or severe gluten sensitivity. The serving spoons, and the proximity to the large flour tortillas, can easily cause cross-contact.

  • 1. Communicate First:

    When you reach the start of the line, clearly state, "I have a **gluten allergy/Celiac** and need an **allergy order**, please." This prompts staff to follow specific safety protocols.

  • 2. Request Glove Change:

    Ask the staff member preparing your food to **change their gloves**. This is the single most important step in preventing cross-contact.

  • 3. Ask for Clean Utensils:

    Request that they use **fresh serving spoons** for all your ingredients, especially the rice and protein, which often come close to the flour tortillas.

  • 4. The "Back" Tip (Highest Safety):

    For the highest level of safety, politely ask the staff to pull your main ingredients from **fresh tubs in the back** that have not been exposed to the service line.

The Few Items to be Mindful Of

Most items are safe, but here are the few to approach with caution:

  • Flour Tortillas: This is the only item that contains gluten. **Avoid** burritos and soft tacos.
  • Chipotle Honey Vinaigrette: Check the current allergen statement, as some dressings may contain gluten.
  • Roasted Chili-Corn Salsa & Chips: Chipotle notes that corn items may have trace amounts of gluten due to "co-mingling with gluten-containing grains in the field." Skip this if you are extremely sensitive.

Conclusion: Control is Key

Chipotle provides excellent building blocks for a gluten-free meal. By opting for a **Burrito Bowl** or **Salad** and clearly communicating your needs for glove changes and fresh utensils, you maintain **control** over the preparation process.

This attention to detail makes Chipotle one of the best and safest bets in the fast-casual landscape.
